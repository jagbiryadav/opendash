# Data Quality Policy

## Purpose
Establish standards and procedures for maintaining data quality across the organization.

## Scope
Applies to all data used for analysis, reporting, and decision-making.

## Policy Statements

### 1. Data Quality Standards
All data must meet the following minimum standards:
- **Completeness**: >95% of required fields populated
- **Accuracy**: >98% of values correct
- **Consistency**: No contradictions across related data
- **Timeliness**: Data updated within defined SLA
- **Validity**: All values conform to defined formats

### 2. Data Quality Responsibilities
- **Data Owners**: Accountable for data quality in their domain
- **Data Stewards**: Responsible for day-to-day quality management
- **Data Users**: Responsible for reporting quality issues
- **Data Engineers**: Responsible for quality checks in pipelines

### 3. Data Quality Monitoring
- Automated quality checks must run on all data pipelines
- Quality metrics must be logged and tracked
- Quality dashboards must be maintained and reviewed weekly
- Quality issues must be documented and tracked to resolution

### 4. Data Quality Remediation
- Critical quality issues must be resolved within 24 hours
- High-severity issues must be resolved within 72 hours
- Medium-severity issues must be resolved within 1 week
- Low-severity issues must be resolved within 1 month

### 5. Data Quality Reporting
- Monthly quality scorecards must be produced
- Quality trends must be analyzed quarterly
- Quality improvements must be documented and communicated

## Enforcement
Violations of this policy may result in:
- Mandatory retraining
- Restricted data access
- Disciplinary action

## Review
This policy is reviewed annually and updated as needed.
