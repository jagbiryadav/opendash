# Business Frameworks Reference

## Strategic Analysis Frameworks

### SWOT Analysis
- **Strengths**: Internal capabilities that provide advantage
- **Weaknesses**: Internal limitations that create disadvantage
- **Opportunities**: External factors that could be exploited
- **Threats**: External factors that could cause problems

**Usage**: Strategic planning, competitive analysis, product development

### PESTEL Analysis
- **Political**: Government policies, regulations, stability
- **Economic**: Growth rates, inflation, exchange rates, unemployment
- **Social**: Demographics, cultural trends, lifestyle changes
- **Technological**: Innovation, R&D, automation, digital trends
- **Environmental**: Climate change, sustainability, regulations
- **Legal**: Employment laws, consumer protection, health & safety

**Usage**: Market entry, strategic planning, risk assessment

### Porter's Five Forces
1. **Threat of new entrants**: Barriers to entry (capital, regulations, brand loyalty)
2. **Bargaining power of suppliers**: Supplier concentration, switching costs
3. **Bargaining power of buyers**: Buyer concentration, price sensitivity
4. **Threat of substitutes**: Alternative products/services
5. **Competitive rivalry**: Number of competitors, industry growth

**Usage**: Industry analysis, competitive strategy, investment decisions

### Value Chain Analysis
- **Primary activities**: Inbound logistics, Operations, Outbound logistics, Marketing & Sales, Service
- **Support activities**: Firm infrastructure, HR management, Technology development, Procurement

**Usage**: Cost optimization, competitive advantage identification, process improvement

### Blue Ocean Strategy
- **Red Ocean**: Compete in existing market space
- **Blue Ocean**: Create uncontested market space
- **Tools**: Strategy Canvas, Four Actions Framework (Eliminate, Reduce, Raise, Create)

**Usage**: Innovation, market creation, differentiation strategy

## Growth Frameworks

### Ansoff Matrix
- **Market Penetration**: Existing products, existing markets
- **Market Development**: Existing products, new markets
- **Product Development**: New products, existing markets
- **Diversification**: New products, new markets

**Usage**: Growth strategy selection, risk assessment

### BCG Growth-Share Matrix
- **Stars**: High growth, high market share (invest)
- **Cash Cows**: Low growth, high market share (harvest)
- **Question Marks**: High growth, low market share (selective investment)
- **Dogs**: Low growth, low market share (divest)

**Usage**: Portfolio management, resource allocation

### AARRR (Pirate Metrics)
- **Acquisition**: How users find you
- **Activation**: First user experience
- **Retention**: Users coming back
- **Revenue**: Monetization
- **Referral**: Users recommending you

**Usage**: Startup metrics, growth optimization, funnel analysis

### North Star Metric
- Single metric that captures core value delivery
- Leading indicator of long-term success
- Aligns entire organization

**Usage**: Strategic focus, team alignment, progress measurement

## Customer Frameworks

### RFM Analysis
- **Recency**: How recently did they purchase?
- **Frequency**: How often do they purchase?
- **Monetary**: How much do they spend?

**Usage**: Customer segmentation, targeted marketing, churn prediction

### Customer Journey Mapping
1. **Awareness**: Customer becomes aware of need
2. **Consideration**: Customer evaluates options
3. **Decision**: Customer makes purchase
4. **Retention**: Customer uses product/service
5. **Advocacy**: Customer recommends to others

**Usage**: UX optimization, marketing strategy, service design

### LTV/CAC Analysis
- **Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)**: Total revenue from customer over relationship
- **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**: Total cost to acquire customer
- **LTV/CAC Ratio**: Target > 3.0 for healthy business

**Usage**: Marketing ROI, sustainability assessment, growth planning

### Cohort Retention Analysis
- Group customers by acquisition date/characteristics
- Track retention over time
- Identify drop-off points
- Compare cohort performance

**Usage**: Product-market fit, retention optimization, valuation

### Net Promoter Score (NPS)
- **Promoters (9-10)**: Loyal enthusiasts
- **Passives (7-8)**: Satisfied but vulnerable
- **Detractors (0-6)**: Unhappy customers
- **NPS = % Promoters - % Detractors**

**Usage**: Customer satisfaction, loyalty measurement, growth prediction

## Financial Frameworks

### Contribution Margin Analysis
- **Contribution Margin** = Revenue - Variable Costs
- **Contribution Margin Ratio** = CM / Revenue
- **Fixed Costs**: Costs that don't change with volume

**Usage**: Pricing decisions, break-even analysis, product mix optimization

### ROI Analysis
- **ROI** = (Net Profit / Cost of Investment) × 100%
- **Simple ROI**: Total return / Total investment
- **Annualized ROI**: Compounded annual growth rate

**Usage**: Investment evaluation, project prioritization, resource allocation

### NPV (Net Present Value)
- **NPV** = Σ(Cash Flow / (1 + r)^t) - Initial Investment
- r = discount rate, t = time period
- NPV > 0: Value-creating investment

**Usage**: Capital budgeting, project evaluation, M&A analysis

### Sensitivity Analysis
- Vary one input at a time
- Measure impact on output
- Identify key drivers
- Tornado diagrams for visualization

**Usage**: Risk assessment, decision-making under uncertainty

### Monte Carlo Simulation
- Define probability distributions for inputs
- Run thousands of simulations
- Generate probability distribution of outputs
- Calculate confidence intervals

**Usage**: Risk quantification, scenario planning, financial forecasting

## Operational Frameworks

### Root Cause Analysis (5 Whys)
1. Why did the problem occur? → Answer 1
2. Why did Answer 1 happen? → Answer 2
3. Why did Answer 2 happen? → Answer 3
4. Why did Answer 3 happen? → Answer 4
5. Why did Answer 4 happen? → Root Cause

**Usage**: Problem solving, process improvement, quality management

### Fishbone Diagram (Ishikawa)
- **Categories**: People, Process, Technology, Materials, Measurement, Environment
- **Root causes**: Branch off main categories
- **Effect**: The problem being analyzed

**Usage**: Quality management, process improvement, team brainstorming

### Pareto Analysis (80/20 Rule)
- 80% of effects come from 20% of causes
- Focus on vital few, not trivial many
- Prioritize improvements by impact

**Usage**: Quality improvement, resource allocation, prioritization

### Decision Trees
- **Root**: Decision or chance node
- **Branches**: Options or outcomes
- **Leaves**: Final outcomes with values
- **Expected Value**: Probability-weighted outcomes

**Usage**: Decision analysis, risk assessment, strategy selection

### Funnel Analysis
- **Top of funnel**: Awareness/visits
- **Middle of funnel**: Engagement/interest
- **Bottom of funnel**: Conversion/purchase
- **Conversion rates**: Between each stage

**Usage**: Marketing optimization, UX improvement, sales process

## Innovation Frameworks

### Design Thinking
1. **Empathize**: Understand user needs
2. **Define**: Frame the problem
3. **Ideate**: Generate solutions
4. **Prototype**: Build representations
5. **Test**: Validate with users

**Usage**: Product design, innovation, user experience

### Lean Startup
- **Build-Measure-Learn** cycle
- **MVP**: Minimum Viable Product
- **Pivot or Persevere**: Based on validated learning
- **Innovation Accounting**: Measure progress

**Usage**: Startup development, product innovation, risk reduction

### Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)
- Customers "hire" products to do "jobs"
- Focus on outcomes, not features
- Functional, emotional, social dimensions

**Usage**: Product development, innovation, marketing messaging

## Data-Driven Frameworks

### A/B Testing Framework
1. **Hypothesis**: What we believe will happen
2. **Metrics**: Primary success metric, guardrails
3. **Sample size**: Power analysis
4. **Randomization**: Proper assignment
5. **Analysis**: Statistical significance + effect size
6. **Decision**: Ship, don't ship, or extend test

**Usage**: Product optimization, marketing testing, UX improvements

### Metric Trees
- **North Star Metric**: Top-level outcome
- **Input Metrics**: Factors we can influence
- **Output Metrics**: Results of our actions
- **Driver Analysis**: Which inputs drive outputs most

**Usage**: Strategic planning, performance management, goal setting

### Cohort Analysis
- Group by acquisition time or characteristics
- Track behavior over time
- Compare across cohorts
- Identify trends and patterns

**Usage**: Retention analysis, product-market fit, customer lifecycle

## Industry-Specific Frameworks

### SaaS Metrics
- **MRR/ARR**: Monthly/Annual Recurring Revenue
- **Churn Rate**: % customers lost per period
- **Expansion Revenue**: Revenue from existing customers
- **Net Revenue Retention**: (Starting MRR + Expansion - Churn) / Starting MRR
- **CAC Payback**: Months to recover acquisition cost
- **Rule of 40**: Growth rate + profit margin > 40%

### E-commerce Metrics
- **Conversion Rate**: Visitors who purchase
- **Average Order Value (AOV)**: Revenue per order
- **Cart Abandonment Rate**: % who add to cart but don't purchase
- **Customer Lifetime Value**: Total revenue per customer
- **Inventory Turnover**: COGS / Average inventory
- **Days Sales of Inventory**: Average days to sell inventory

### Marketing Metrics
- **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**: Marketing spend / New customers
- **Marketing ROI**: (Revenue from marketing - Marketing cost) / Marketing cost
- **Click-Through Rate (CTR)**: Clicks / Impressions
- **Cost Per Click (CPC)**: Ad spend / Clicks
- **Conversion Rate**: Conversions / Visitors
- **Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)**: Revenue / Ad spend

### Financial Metrics
- **Gross Margin**: (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue
- **Operating Margin**: Operating income / Revenue
- **Net Profit Margin**: Net income / Revenue
- **Current Ratio**: Current assets / Current liabilities
- **Debt-to-Equity**: Total debt / Total equity
- **Return on Equity (ROE)**: Net income / Shareholder equity
