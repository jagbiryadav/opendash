# Consulting Frameworks

## MECE (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive)

**Purpose**: Structure problems without overlap or gaps

**When to Use**:
- Decomposing complex business problems
- Creating issue trees
- Organizing analysis

**Application**:
```
Revenue Decline
├── Volume Decline
│   ├── Fewer customers
│   └── Less purchases per customer
└── Price Decline
    ├── Lower prices
    └── Product mix shift
```

**Rules**:
- Categories must not overlap (Mutually Exclusive)
- Categories must cover all possibilities (Collectively Exhaustive)
- Each level must be MECE

## 5 Whys

**Purpose**: Root cause analysis through iterative questioning

**When to Use**:
- Diagnostic problems
- Understanding causation
- Finding underlying issues

**Application**:
```
Problem: Sales dropped 18%
→ Why? Customers bought less
→ Why? Customer satisfaction declined
→ Why? Product quality issues
→ Why? Supplier quality degraded
→ Why? Supplier changed manufacturing process
Root Cause: Supplier process change
```

**Rules**:
- Keep asking "Why?" until you reach a process/system level cause
- Usually 5 levels deep
- Stop when you reach an actionable cause

## Fishbone Diagram (Ishikawa)

**Purpose**: Visualize potential causes of a problem

**Categories**:
- **People**: Skills, training, turnover
- **Process**: Procedures, workflows, policies
- **Technology**: Systems, tools, infrastructure
- **Data**: Quality, availability, accuracy
- **Environment**: Market, competition, regulation
- **Materials**: Suppliers, inventory, quality

**When to Use**:
- Brainstorming potential causes
- Organizing root cause analysis
- Communicating causes to stakeholders

---

## SWOT Analysis

**Purpose**: Strategic assessment of internal and external factors

**Components**:
- **Strengths**: Internal advantages
- **Weaknesses**: Internal limitations
- **Opportunities**: External possibilities
- **Threats**: External risks

**Application**:
```
Strengths              Weaknesses
- Strong brand         - High costs
- Loyal customers      - Old technology
- Market leader        - Slow innovation

Opportunities          Threats
- New markets          - New competitors
- Digital trends       - Regulation
- Partnerships         - Economic downturn
```

**When to Use**:
- Strategic planning
- Competitive analysis
- Business case development

---

## PESTLE Analysis

**Purpose**: External environment scanning

**Components**:
- **Political**: Government policies, stability
- **Economic**: Growth, inflation, interest rates
- **Social**: Demographics, trends, culture
- **Technological**: Innovation, disruption
- **Legal**: Regulations, compliance
- **Environmental**: Sustainability, climate

**When to Use**:
- Market entry decisions
- Strategic planning
- Risk assessment

---

## Porter's Five Forces

**Purpose**: Industry competitive analysis

**Forces**:
1. **Threat of New Entrants**: Barriers to entry
2. **Bargaining Power of Suppliers**: Supplier concentration
3. **Bargaining Power of Buyers**: Buyer concentration
4. **Threat of Substitutes**: Alternative products
5. **Competitive Rivalry**: Number and strength of competitors

**When to Use**:
- Industry analysis
- Competitive positioning
- Investment decisions

---

## RFM Analysis (Recency, Frequency, Monetary)

**Purpose**: Customer segmentation based on behavior

**Components**:
- **Recency**: How recently did they purchase?
- **Frequency**: How often do they purchase?
- **Monetary**: How much do they spend?

**Segmentation**:
```
Champions:      Recent, Frequent, High spend
Loyal:          Frequent, High spend
At Risk:       曾经 Frequent, now inactive
Lost:           Long inactive, low spend
New:            Recent, low frequency
```

**When to Use**:
- Customer retention
- Marketing campaigns
- Churn prediction

---

## AARRR (Pirate Metrics)

**Purpose**: Startup growth metrics framework

**Metrics**:
1. **Acquisition**: How do users find you?
2. **Activation**: Do users have a good first experience?
3. **Retention**: Do users come back?
4. **Revenue**: Do users pay?
5. **Referral**: Do users recommend you?

**When to Use**:
- Startup analysis
- Growth optimization
- Funnel analysis

---

## HEART Framework

**Purpose**: User experience measurement

**Components**:
- **Happiness**: Satisfaction, surveys
- **Engagement**: Time on site, interactions
- **Adoption**: New users, feature usage
- **Retention**: Return visits, churn
- **Task Success**: Completion rate, errors

**When to Use**:
- UX research
- Product optimization
- Feature prioritization

---

## North Star Metric

**Purpose**: Single metric that captures core value

**Examples**:
- Airbnb: Nights booked
- Spotify: Time listened
- Slack: Messages sent
- Facebook: Daily active users

**Rules**:
- Must measure core value delivered
- Must be actionable
- Must be measurable
- Must align with business model

---

## Balanced Scorecard

**Purpose**: Performance measurement across perspectives

**Perspectives**:
1. **Financial**: Revenue, profit, ROI
2. **Customer**: Satisfaction, retention, acquisition
3. **Internal Processes**: Efficiency, quality, cycle time
4. **Learning & Growth**: Training, innovation, culture

**When to Use**:
- Performance management
- Strategy execution
- Balanced decision making

---

## Growth Framework

**Purpose**: Identify growth levers

**Equation**:
```
Growth = Traffic × Conversion × Retention × Revenue
```

**Levers**:
- **Traffic**: Marketing, SEO, partnerships
- **Conversion**: UX, pricing, trust
- **Retention**: Product value, engagement
- **Revenue**: Pricing, upselling, cross-selling

**When to Use**:
- Growth strategy
- Resource allocation
- Performance optimization

---

## Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)

**Purpose**: Understand customer needs through "jobs"

**Concept**: Customers "hire" products to do "jobs"

**Application**:
```
Job: "Help me feel safe when driving"
Solution: Car with safety features
Not: "Sell me a car with airbags"
```

**When to Use**:
- Product development
- Innovation
- Customer research

---

## Value Proposition Canvas

**Purpose**: Align product with customer needs

**Components**:
- **Customer Profile**: Jobs, pains, gains
- **Value Map**: Products, pain relievers, gain creators

**When to Use**:
- Product design
- Marketing messaging
- Competitive positioning

---

## Ansoff Matrix

**Purpose**: Growth strategy classification

**Quadrants**:
- **Market Penetration**: Existing products, existing markets
- **Market Development**: Existing products, new markets
- **Product Development**: New products, existing markets
- **Diversification**: New products, new markets

**When to Use**:
- Strategic planning
- Growth decisions
- Risk assessment

---

## BCG Matrix

**Purpose**: Portfolio analysis

**Quadrants**:
- **Stars**: High growth, high market share
- **Cash Cows**: Low growth, high market share
- **Question Marks**: High growth, low market share
- **Dogs**: Low growth, low market share

**When to Use**:
- Portfolio management
- Resource allocation
- Investment decisions

---

## GE-McKinsey Matrix

**Purpose**: Portfolio prioritization

**Factors**:
- **Industry Attractiveness**: Market size, growth, profitability
- **Competitive Strength**: Market share, brand, capabilities

**When to Use**:
- Portfolio decisions
- Investment prioritization
- Strategic planning

---

## McKinsey 7S

**Purpose**: Organizational effectiveness

**Components**:
- **Strategy**: Plan to achieve competitive advantage
- **Structure**: Organization design
- **Systems**: Daily activities and processes
- **Shared Values**: Core culture
- **Skills**: Core competencies
- **Staff**: People and capabilities
- **Style**: Leadership approach

**When to Use**:
- Organizational change
- Mergers and acquisitions
- Culture assessment

---

## OKR (Objectives and Key Results)

**Purpose**: Goal setting and alignment

**Structure**:
```
Objective: Increase customer satisfaction
Key Results:
- NPS score from 40 to 60
- Response time from 24h to 4h
- Resolution rate from 80% to 95%
```

**When to Use**:
- Goal setting
- Performance management
- Alignment
