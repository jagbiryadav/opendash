# Counterfactual Thinking & Opportunity Analysis

## Counterfactual Reasoning

### What Is Counterfactual Thinking?
Asking "What if X had been different?" to understand causation and quantify impact.

### Framework
```
Observed Outcome
↓
Identify What Changed
↓
Model Alternative Scenario
↓
Calculate Difference
↓
Quantify Impact
↓
Recommend Action
```

### Example: Marketing Spend Reduced

**Observed**: Revenue dropped 18%

**Counterfactual Question**: What if marketing spend had remained constant?

**Reasoning**:
```
Observed:
- Marketing spend: -30%
- Revenue: -18%

Historical relationship:
- Every 10% marketing change → 5% revenue change (elasticity = 0.5)

Counterfactual:
- If marketing had stayed constant
- Revenue would have been: -18% + (30% × 0.5) = -3%
- Marketing reduction caused: -15% of the -18% decline
- Other factors caused: -3%
```

**Conclusion**: Marketing reduction explains 83% of the decline.

### Example: Price Increase

**Observed**: Units sold dropped 12% after 10% price increase

**Counterfactual**: What if we hadn't raised prices?

**Reasoning**:
```
Observed:
- Price: +10%
- Units: -12%
- Revenue: -3.2% (1.1 × 0.88 = 0.968)

Counterfactual:
- If price stayed constant
- Units would have been stable (assuming no other changes)
- Revenue would have been: same

Impact of price increase:
- Revenue loss: -3.2%
- But margin improved: +10% price vs -12% volume
- Net profit impact depends on cost structure
```

### Example: Feature Launch

**Observed**: Churn decreased after feature launch

**Counterfactual**: What if we hadn't launched the feature?

**Reasoning**:
```
Observed:
- Churn: 5% → 3% (40% reduction)
- Feature adoption: 60% of users

Counterfactual:
- If feature not launched
- Churn would likely have remained at 5%
- Or increased due to competition

Impact:
- Feature prevented: 2% churn reduction
- Affected: 60% of users
- Saved customers: 60% × 2% = 1.2% of base
```

## Opportunity Analysis

### Framework
```
Positive Outcome
↓
Understand Why It Happened
↓
Assess Replicability
↓
Estimate Potential
↓
Calculate ROI
↓
Recommend Investment
```

### Example: Sales Increased

**Observation**: Sales increased 25% in Q3

**Analysis**:
```
Why?
- New marketing campaign
- Seasonal boost
- Competitive weakness

Replicability:
- Marketing campaign: High (can repeat)
- Seasonal: Low (natural variation)
- Competitive: Medium (depends on competitor)

Potential:
- Marketing: Can invest more, expect proportional return
- Seasonal: Plan for Q3 peaks
- Competitive: Monitor and react

ROI Estimate:
- Additional marketing spend: $100K
- Expected return: $250K (2.5x ROI)
- Recommendation: Increase marketing budget
```

### Example: Feature Adoption Spike

**Observation**: New feature adoption jumped 40%

**Analysis**:
```
Why?
- Product-market fit
- Marketing push
- Competitive gap

Replicability:
- Product-market fit: High (core value)
- Marketing: High (can repeat)
- Competitive gap: Low (temporary)

Potential:
- Expand feature to more users
- Build complementary features
- Use as competitive advantage

ROI Estimate:
- Development cost: $50K
- Expected revenue increase: $200K
- Recommendation: Double down on feature
```

## Scenario Planning

### Framework
```
Base Case (Most Likely)
↓
Best Case (Optimistic)
↓
Worst Case (Pessimistic)
↓
Compare Outcomes
↓
Identify Key Drivers
↓
Plan Mitigations
```

### Example: Market Expansion

**Base Case** (50% probability):
- Market share: 5%
- Revenue: $10M
- Profit: $2M

**Best Case** (25% probability):
- Market share: 8%
- Revenue: $16M
- Profit: $4M

**Worst Case** (25% probability):
- Market share: 2%
- Revenue: $4M
- Profit: -$1M

**Key Drivers**:
- Competitive response
- Customer adoption rate
- Execution quality

**Mitigations**:
- Phased rollout
- Competitive monitoring
- Contingency budget

## Risk-Adjusted Recommendations

### Framework
```
Recommendation
↓
Identify Risks
↓
Assess Probability
↓
Estimate Impact
↓
Calculate Expected Value
↓
Adjust Recommendation
```

### Example: Price Increase

**Recommendation**: Increase prices by 10%

**Risks**:
- Customer churn: 30% probability, -20% revenue impact
- Competitive response: 20% probability, -10% revenue impact
- Brand damage: 10% probability, -5% revenue impact

**Expected Value**:
```
Base case: +10% revenue
Risk-adjusted: 
- 70% × +10% = +7%
- 30% × -20% = -6%
- 20% × -10% = -2%
- 10% × -5% = -0.5%
Net expected: -1.5%
```

**Adjusted Recommendation**: Don't increase prices unless brand strength can absorb risk.

## Application in Analysis

### Step 1: Identify What Happened
Revenue dropped 18%

### Step 2: Counterfactual Question
What if marketing spend had remained constant?

### Step 3: Model Alternative
With historical elasticity, revenue would have dropped only 3%

### Step 4: Quantify Impact
Marketing reduction caused 15% of the 18% decline

### Step 5: Opportunity Analysis
What if we restore marketing?

### Step 6: Estimate ROI
Restore marketing → Expected 12% revenue recovery → $X impact

### Step 7: Risk Assessment
Risks: Seasonal factors, competitive response

### Step 8: Recommendation
Restore marketing with monitoring
